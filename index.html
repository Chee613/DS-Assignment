The Context

Problem Statement

Used car pricing is extremely opaque. Buyers and sellers struggle to agree on a "fair market value" due to significant information asymmetry and:

  • Rapid Depreciation Uncertainty: Buyers are unsure if the prestige of a luxury badge is worth the steeper loss in future resale value compared to mass-market brands.
  • Geospatial Price Variance: It is unclear if purchasing the same vehicle in a different state (e.g., Johor vs. Kuala Lumpur) offers significant cost savings due to local supply-demand dynamics.
  • The "Origin" Premium: The market lacks data on whether the perceived quality of imported (CBU) units translates into a tangible, long-term price advantage over locally assembled (CKD) units.

This project solves this by rigorously testing these market hypotheses to build a data-driven Price Prediction Engine

Dataset Overview

The primary data source for this research is the malaysia_resale_car.csv, a curated collection of vehicle listings capturing the nuances of the local market.

View on Kaggle

Sample Size

952 unique car listings meticulously cleaned for missing values and currency format errors.

Feature Scope

Features include Brand, List Price, Vehicle Age, Mileage, Engine CC, Location

Temporal Range

Covers vehicles from recent model years back to legacy staples, providing a comprehensive depreciation curve.

Data Quality

Preprocessing: Cleaning involved parsing currency, converting mileage ranges to averages, and calculating "Car Age" based on a 2022 baseline and finding Assembly Status (CBU vs. CKD).

Insights (Asking Questions)

We tested three core assumptions that define the Malaysian market landscape.

H1 Analysis

The "Luxury Badge Tax"

"Do continental cars depreciate faster than Asian cars?"

Result: Bivariate regression confirms Continental cars lose value significantly faster (steeper slope) than Asian cars.

H2 Analysis

Regional Disparity

"Does location influence car price due to demand?"

Result: Regional mapping shows distinct price variations; urban hubs like Kuala Lumpur show significantly higher valuation density.

H3 Analysis

The Assembly Premium

"Do CBU/Recon cars retain value better than CKD?"

Result: Data confirms a sustained price premium for CBU/Recon units, validating high consumer trust in imported builds.

Machine Learning Models & Methods

Target Transformation

Log-Transformation applied to price to handle high right-skewness (Skew > 3.0), ensuring the models aren't biased by outliers.

Random Forest Regressor

Utilized to capture non-linear relationships such as mileage thresholds, age curves, and brand prestige factors.

Multiple Linear Regression

Used to determine specific coefficients and weights of individual features, providing high interpretability.

Summary

Conclusion

This project developed a pricing engine for the Malaysian used car market with 90.9% accuracy, outperforming the baseline model by reducing prediction error by over RM 11,000 per vehicle.

The analysis validated all three core hypotheses:

  • Depreciation: Continental car brands exhibit a significantly faster rate of depreciation compared to Asian car brands.

  • Regionality: Listing prices for identical models are significantly higher in economic hubs (KL/Selangor) compared to Johor.

  • Assembly: Complete Built-Up (CBU) vehicles exhibit a flatter depreciation slope compared to locally assembled (CKD) models.

90.86% Accuracy

The model effectively explains over 90% of price variance in the current market.
