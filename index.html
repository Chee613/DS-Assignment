The Context

Problem Statement

Used car pricing is extremely opaque. Buyers and sellers struggle to agree on a "fair market value" due to significant information asymmetry and:

  • Engine Capacity AnchoringThere is a lack of clarity regarding whether a larger engine displacement fundamentally justifies a higher price ceiling or if it is merely a proxy for the vehicle's luxury segment.
  • Geospatial Price Variance: It is unclear if purchasing the same vehicle in a different state (e.g., Johor vs. Kuala Lumpur) offers significant cost savings due to local sup
  • Rapid Depreciation Uncertainty: Buyers are unsure if the prestige of a luxury badge is worth the steeper loss in future resale value compared to mass-market brands.

This project solves this by rigorously testing these market hypotheses to build a data-driven Car Price Prediction Engine

Dataset Overview

The primary data source for this research is the carlist_scraped_data.csv, a curated collection of vehicle listings capturing the nuances of the local market.

View Dataset

Sample Size

4000 unique car listings meticulously cleaned for missing values and currency format errors.

Feature Scope

Features include Brand, List Price, Vehicle Age, Mileage, Engine CC, Location

Temporal Range

Covers vehicles from recent model years back to legacy staples, providing a comprehensive depreciation curve.

Data Quality

Preprocessing: Cleaning involved parsing currency, converting mileage ranges to averages, calculating "Car Age" based on a 2025 baseline, data extraction from description(year, brand, origin) and location generalisation.

Insights (Asking Questions)

We tested three core assumptions that define the Malaysian market landscape.

H1 Analysis

Engine Capacity Anchoring

"Do cars with larger engine displacements influence car price? continental cars depreciate faster than Asian cars?"

Result:Cars with large engine displacements (>2.4L) exhibit a higher median price compared to small engine (<1.6L).

H2 Analysis

Regional Disparity

"Does location influence car price due to demand?"

Result: Regional mapping shows distinct price variations; urban hubs like Kuala Lumpur show significantly higher valuation density.

H3 Analysis

The "Luxury Badge Tax"

"Do continental cars depreciate faster than Asian cars?"

Result: Multivariate regression confirms Continental cars lose value significantly faster (steeper slope) than Asian cars.

Variable Correlation

A correlation analysis confirmed our predictors' impact on Price:

Strongest Predictors: Engine Size (+0.50), Mileage (-0.41) with Year/Car Age (0.28) following close behind, indicating that physical capacity and wear-and-tear drive value more than calendar age.

Key Insight: Engine Size effectively sets the initial "Price Ceiling" (Luxury vs. Budget), while Mileage is the primary force determining how far a vehicle's value drops from that peak.

Machine Learning Models & Methods

Target Transformation

Log-Transformation applied to price to handle high right-skewness (Skew > 3.0), ensuring the models aren't biased by outliers.

Random Forest Regressor

Utilized to capture non-linear relationships such as mileage thresholds, age curves, and brand prestige factors.

Multiple Linear Regression

Used to determine specific coefficients and weights of individual features, providing high interpretability.

Summary

Conclusion

This project developed a pricing engine for the Malaysian used car market with 88.26% accuracy, outperforming the baseline model by reducing prediction error by over RM 10,784 per vehicle.

The analysis validated all three core hypotheses:

  • Engine Size: Cars with larger engine displacements exhibit a higher price ceiling compared to smaller displacement models

  • Regionality: Listing prices for identical models are higher in economic hubs (KL) compared to Perak abd Negeri Sembilan.

  • Depreciation: Continental car brands exhibit a faster rate of depreciation compared to Asian car brands.

88.26% Accuracy

The model effectively explains approximately 88% of price variance within the current used car market.
